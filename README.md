Title: A New Idea I’m Exploring: Could “AI Business Cards” Become the Future Entry Point for Personal AI?

Recently I’ve been thinking about a question:

Today, people have personal profiles, resumes, portfolios, GitHub pages, and personal websites. But all of these mainly show what a person has done in the past.

If everyone may have their own AI assistant in the future, should there also be a new way to present that AI identity?

I’m calling this concept an **AI Business Card**.

To me, an AI Business Card is not just a personal bio, and it is not simply a chatbot link. It should be a structured identity system for an AI.

It should answer questions such as:

1. What level is this AI?
2. What level of task complexity can it handle?
3. What capability modules does it specialize in?
4. What project outcomes can it help deliver?
5. What kinds of tasks can users confidently delegate to it?

The inspiration is not to literally make it look like a credit card or a military rank. Instead, I’m borrowing the underlying logic behind them:

Credit cards communicate identity tiers, rarity, material difference, and privilege.

Military ranks communicate capability level, responsibility, authority, and scope.

An AI Business Card could use a similar logic to express an AI’s capability level, identity, boundaries, and delivery potential.

For example, AI capabilities could be divided into several levels:

**L1: Information AI**
Mainly handles Q&A, summarization, explanation, and translation.

**L2: Task AI**
Can complete clear small tasks, such as writing documents, organizing plans, or generating simple code.

**L3: Professional AI**
Has domain-specific analytical ability, such as embedded systems, IoT, frontend engineering, product design, or interview coaching.

**L4: Solution AI**
Can break down complex problems into requirements, architecture, modules, roadmaps, and testing plans.

**L5: Project AI**
Can participate in full project planning from 0 to 1, including execution assistance, code generation, version iteration, and commercialization analysis.

I think the most important thing about an AI Business Card is not to show “this AI has many features.”

Instead, it should first define the AI’s identity, level, capability boundary, and delivery scope.

The structure might look like this:

AI level
→ AI identity positioning
→ What it knows
→ Project delivery capability
→ Tasks users can start directly

For example, if an AI is positioned as an “L5 Project AI,” it is not just answering questions. It may help with things like:

* Designing AI coding tools
* Planning embedded GUI tools
* Designing IoT / energy EMS solutions
* Architecting AI Agent workspaces
* Productizing mini games or mini programs
* Packaging technical projects for interviews and career growth

I’m curious about a few questions:

First, what matters more for an AI Business Card: showing the AI’s identity level, or showing its specific features?

Second, how should AI capability levels be defined? By task complexity, professional depth, or deliverable outcomes?

Third, will everyone eventually need their own AI Business Card, just like people today need resumes, portfolios, and personal websites?

Fourth, if this concept becomes real, is an AI Business Card more like a personal brand entry point, an AI Agent entry point, or a digital twin entry point?

Would love to hear how others think about this direction.
